# M18 Cyclone Plinko — Carnival Game Evaluation

## Scoring (1–10 scale)

### Overall Entertainment: 9/10
Two players wielding actual Milwaukee M18 blowers to blast balls through a plinko field is inherently thrilling. The combination of plinko randomness with active air control creates a game that's never the same twice. The physicality of moving between air ports and the visceral feeling of pulling the trigger on a real power tool puts this in a different league from passive carnival games. The only knock: younger kids (K-1st) may struggle with the blower weight and the multi-port strategy.

### Memorability: 10/10
"Remember that game where we got to use REAL power tools to blast balls?" — that's the sentence every kid says in the car ride home. No other carnival booth has this. The Milwaukee Red aesthetic, the sound of blowers firing, the visible air effects — it hits every sense. This is the game that defines the carnival.

### Ease of Construction: 7/10
The base plinko board is straightforward lumber + acrylic work — two weekends for a pair of dads with (fittingly) M18 tools. The air port funnels need either a 3D printer or some sheet metal work, but they're simple cones. The biggest complexity is getting the air port gaskets right so blower nozzles seat snugly. No electronics, no wiring, no motors. The M18 tools are plug-and-play. Teardown/setup is designed for ~5 minutes with wing nut joints.

### Skill to Luck Spectrum: 8.5/10 (Great Balance)
- **Luck (40%):** Plinko peg deflections are inherently random. Where the ball bounces is chaos. Drop slot choice adds minor skill/luck mix.
- **Skill (60%):** Choosing which air port to use, timing the blast to catch the ball at the right moment, reading the ball's trajectory — this is real skill. Experienced players WILL score higher. But a lucky bounce can always upset the favorite.
- **The Cyclone Factor:** When both players blast simultaneously from opposite sides, skill goes out the window and pure chaos takes over. This is a feature, not a bug.

### Chaos Level: 9/10
- Balls ricocheting off pegs: chaotic
- 120 MPH air blasts deflecting balls mid-flight: very chaotic
- Two opposing air blasts creating a cyclone: absolute chaos
- Multiple balls in the field simultaneously (advanced mode): pandemonium
- The sound of M18 blowers firing adds audio chaos to the visual chaos

Just enough structure (gravity, scoring pockets, turn order) to keep it from being random noise. The chaos is DIRECTED chaos — it has purpose and drama.

### Durability: 8/10
- **Board:** ¾" plywood + acrylic — built to survive years of carnivals. Pegs are glued and bolted.
- **M18 Tools:** Milwaukee builds for construction sites. A carnival is a vacation for these tools.
- **Air ports:** 3D-printed funnels are the weakest link but can be reprinted overnight. Keep spares.
- **Balls:** Whiffle balls are $8/dozen. Disposable by design.
- **Scoring pockets:** Wood dividers may loosen from repeated ball impacts. Use wood glue + screws.
- **Batteries:** M18 XC5.0 batteries run a blower for ~20 min continuous. With 4-6 batteries on rotation, you'll never run dry across a full carnival day.

### Photogenicity: 9.5/10
- Kids holding Milwaukee blowers = instant photo op
- The red/black Milwaukee color scheme against the dark board is visually striking from 20 feet away
- M18 Tower Light on top acts as a literal beacon — draws eyes across the carnival
- Action shots of balls mid-flight with visible air distortion
- The board itself looks like a professional installation, not a DIY booth
- Only missing a half point because the acrylic creates some glare in flash photography

### Onboarding Time: 9/10 (Very Fast)
**Time to explain: ~15 seconds**
1. "Drop your ball in a slot"
2. "Jam the blower in a port and pull the trigger to push your ball"
3. "Get it in the middle pocket for 100 points"

That's it. A kid watching one round understands the game completely. The blower is intuitive — point it where you want air to go. The only learning curve is port selection timing, which comes naturally after one round.

Compare: Cornhole is ~5 seconds to explain. This is ~15. Still nearly instant. D&D is 30+ minutes. This is firmly on the cornhole end of the spectrum.

## Verdict

M18 Cyclone Plinko is the game that turns a school carnival into a legend. The Milwaukee tools aren't a gimmick — they're the core mechanic, the visual identity, and the power source all in one. No outlet, no extension cord, no cheap bouncy house blower. Just two kids, two M18 blowers, and a board full of chaos.

The construction is approachable for two motivated dads with a garage and a couple of weekends. The materials are hardware store standard. The M18 tools most carnival families already own (or will want to after this).

**Build this one.**
