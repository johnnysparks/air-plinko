# Tornado Alley v2 — Build Plan

## Overall Dimensions

| Dimension | Size | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Length | 60" (5 ft) | Two players face off at each end |
| Width | 36" (3 ft) | Wide enough for town layout + boom arm sweep |
| Wall Height | 8" | Keeps ping pong balls in, allows wind to enter from above |
| Table Height | 30" | Standard table height — kids stand, adults lean |
| Boom Post Height | 48" above table | Tall enough for nozzle to angle down into arena |
| Total Footprint | 60"L × 36"W × 78"H (with boom posts) |

## Architecture

### 1. Table Base
- **Tabletop:** 3/4" plywood, 60×36", painted matte black with white/yellow road grid lines (Main Street lengthwise + 3 cross streets)
- **Legs:** 4× folding banquet table legs OR sawhorses — 30" height
- **Edge Walls:** 1×10 lumber on all four sides, forming an 8" deep open-top arena
- **Corner Gussets:** Plywood triangles at each corner for rigidity
- **Storm Shelter Cutouts:** 6 rectangular openings cut into the tabletop (see Section 4)

### 2. Boom Arm System (×2)
Each player gets one boom arm mounted on a vertical post at their end of the table.

**Per boom arm:**
- **Post:** 1.5" PVC pipe, 48" tall, mounted to the outside of the end wall with pipe flange + screws
- **Pivot:** PVC tee fitting at the top of the post — allows 180° horizontal rotation
- **Arm:** 1" PVC pipe, 36" long, inserted through the tee. Extends over the playing field
- **Counterweight:** 1 lb ankle weight clamped to the rear end of the arm (balances the hose weight)
- **Nozzle mount:** 1" PVC elbow at the field end of the arm — points the nozzle downward at ~60° angle
- **Tilt adjustment:** The nozzle end can swing down (gravity) or be lifted by the player — provides vertical aiming
- **Movement range:** Full horizontal sweep across 90% of the table width, ~60% of the table length (can't reach opponent's near end)
- **Overlap zone:** The center ~20% of the table is reachable by BOTH boom arms — this is the contested dead zone

**Nozzle:**
- 3D-printed cone reducer: 4" inlet (fits flex hose) to 1.5" outlet
- Pistol grip handle zip-tied below the nozzle with a squeeze-operated inline ball valve
- Squeeze trigger = air flows. Release = stops. Continuous, instant response

### 3. Blower System
- **Blowers:** 2× 450W bounce house inflators ($25-30 each), one per player
- **Hose:** 6ft of 4" flex duct hose per blower, running from blower on ground up through the boom arm to the nozzle
- **Power:** Standard 120V outlet × 2 (or one 15A circuit with a power strip — total draw ~900W)
- **Damper valve:** Inline butterfly damper on each hose, accessible to operator, for tuning wind power
- **Blower position:** On the ground behind each player's end, hidden behind the table

### 4. Storm Shelters (Scoring Pockets)
- **Construction:** Rectangular cutouts in the tabletop (2.5"–4" wide × 3" deep) with a short ramp leading down into a collection tray underneath
- **One-way flap:** Thin flexible plastic strip (cut from a plastic folder) hinged at the top of the opening. Ball pushes through, flap springs back. Wind can't eject a scored ball
- **Flag indicator:** When a ball drops into the shelter, it rolls onto a small lever that pops up a colored flag (wire + foam flag) through a slot next to the shelter. Visual confirmation of a score from across the table
- **Collection tray:** Shallow box under the table — one per shelter, pull-out for ball retrieval between rounds

### 5. Town Buildings (Wind Obstacles)
All buildings are **scrap wood blocks and hardware store parts** — no 3D printing required for any building. Paint them to look like a small town.

| Building | Construction | Size (L×W×H) | Wind Effect |
|----------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Water Tower | Soup can on 3 dowel legs, painted silver | 3" dia × 6"H | Cylindrical — wind splits around it, dead zone behind |
| General Store | 2×4 block, painted with awning (card stock) | 5×3×3" | Largest wind shadow on the field |
| Church + Steeple | 2×4 block + dowel + triangle cap (cardboard) | 3×3×5" | Steeple deflects downward wind upward |
| Houses ×4 | 2×2" scrap blocks, painted various colors | 2×2×2" | Minor deflection, create channels |
| Fences ×6 | Paint stir sticks, cut with gaps every 1" | 4"L × 1.5"H | Block rolling balls, pass air over top |
| Trees ×4 | 1" dowel in a wood base, pom-pom or foam ball top | 1.5" dia × 3"H | Random deflection, unpredictable |

**Mounting:** Each building has a short dowel pin (1/4" × 1") glued to the bottom that drops into a pre-drilled hole in the tabletop. Removable for reconfiguration and storage. Drill 20+ holes in a grid pattern so buildings can be rearranged between events.

### 6. Wind Visualization Elements

**Ribbon Streamers:**
- **Material:** Surveyor's flagging tape (fluorescent pink/orange, ~$3/roll, hundreds of feet)
- **Length:** 8" strips
- **Mounting:** Stapled or tacked to the top of each building
- **Count:** 2-3 per building = ~40 streamers total
- **Effect:** At rest, they droop. Under wind, they snap horizontal and flutter. Shows wind direction and intensity in real time

**Pinwheels:**
- **Type:** Metallic Mylar party pinwheels ($0.50 each, party supply store)
- **Mounting:** Push the stick into a small hole in fence posts or building corners
- **Count:** 12
- **Effect:** Spin up when wind passes. Metallic surface catches light = visible flashing from distance

**Debris Field:**
- **Materials:** Mixed bag of confetti, small foam craft leaves, tissue paper scraps, lightweight craft pom-poms
- **Prep:** Operator scatters 1-2 handfuls across the table before each round
- **Effect:** Lifts, swirls, scatters when wind hits. Pure spectacle. Zero gameplay function. Contained by 8" walls (mostly)
- **Cleanup:** Quick sweep between rounds. Debris is cheap — replace each round

**Corner Wind Socks:**
- 12" ribbon streamers at each corner of the arena, mounted on small poles
- Show overall airflow direction — players can read the wind at a glance

### 7. Optional Upgrades

**Fog Machine (Tier 2 — $25):**
- Small 400W fog machine, positioned at the midpoint of one long wall
- 5-second burst at the start of each round fills the arena with low-lying fog (~3" deep)
- Blowers carve visible paths through the fog — the single most spectacular effect
- Fog dissipates over 60 seconds, creating a natural visual arc
- **Fluid cost:** ~$8/bottle, lasts 50+ rounds
- **Note:** Fog sets off smoke detectors — outdoor/tent booth only

**UV Blacklight (Tier 3 — $15):**
- 2× 24" UV LED strip lights, zip-tied to the boom arm posts, aimed down
- Fluorescent ping pong balls (neon), flagging tape, and white confetti all glow under UV
- Best effect at dusk or in a shaded/tented booth
- Combined with fog = glowing storm table visible from 50+ feet

**Tornado Siren (Tier 0 — $0):**
- Bluetooth speaker playing a tornado siren sound at round start/end
- Free tornado siren sounds from any phone
- Adds enormous atmosphere for zero cost

## Build Sequence

### Phase 1: Table (Day 1, ~3 hours)
1. Cut plywood tabletop to 60×36"
2. Cut and attach 1×10 edge walls on all four sides (screws + glue)
3. Add corner gussets
4. Attach folding leg brackets and legs (or set on sawhorses)
5. Paint tabletop matte black
6. Mask and paint white/yellow road grid (Main Street + cross streets)
7. Drill 20+ dowel pin holes in a grid pattern for building mounts
8. Cut 6 storm shelter openings in the tabletop
9. Build and attach under-table collection trays

### Phase 2: Buildings & Shelters (Day 1, ~2 hours)
1. Cut scrap wood blocks for all buildings
2. Paint buildings (red barn, white church, silver water tower, colored houses)
3. Glue dowel pins to building bottoms
4. Build church steeple (dowel + cardboard triangle)
5. Build water tower (soup can + dowel legs)
6. Cut fence sections from paint stir sticks (cut gap notches)
7. Install one-way flaps on shelter openings
8. Install flag pop-up levers (or skip — flags can be manual)
9. Place all buildings, test layout

### Phase 3: Boom Arms & Blowers (Day 2, ~3 hours)
1. Cut and assemble PVC boom posts (1.5" pipe + flange + tee at top)
2. Cut and assemble PVC boom arms (1" pipe + elbow at field end)
3. Mount boom posts to outside of table end walls
4. Attach 3D-printed nozzle cone reducers to boom arm ends
5. Attach pistol grip handles with squeeze valve
6. Run flex hose from ground-level blowers up through boom arm to nozzle
7. Install inline damper valves
8. Clamp counterweights to rear boom arm ends
9. Power on and tune — adjust damper until ping pong ball rolls ~2-3 ft/sec on open tabletop

### Phase 4: Wind Effects & Polish (Day 2, ~1 hour)
1. Cut 40+ strips of flagging tape (8" each), staple to building rooftops
2. Mount pinwheels on fence posts and building corners
3. Build corner wind sock poles
4. Test with blowers — verify streamers respond visibly, pinwheels spin
5. Scatter debris, test visual effect
6. Optional: set up fog machine, test fill-and-carve cycle
7. Optional: mount UV strips, test glow effect
8. Full playtest: 12 balls (6 red, 6 blue), two players, 60 seconds

## Operator Guide (Day-Of)

### Setup (5 minutes)
1. Unfold table legs / set on sawhorses
2. Drop buildings into dowel holes (pre-marked layout taped inside the wall)
3. Snap boom arm posts into flanges (2 wing nuts each)
4. Slide boom arms through tee fittings
5. Connect flex hoses to blowers
6. Plug in blowers (verify both run)
7. One test blast per side to check damper settings

### Between Rounds (30 seconds)
1. Pull out shelter collection trays, dump balls into bowl
2. Re-scatter 6 red + 6 blue balls across Main Street
3. Scatter fresh debris handful
4. Optional: 5-second fog burst
5. Hand nozzles to next players

### Teardown (10 minutes)
1. Unplug blowers
2. Disconnect hoses
3. Remove boom arms and posts (4 wing nuts)
4. Pull all buildings from dowel holes, bag them
5. Fold table legs
6. Everything fits in a minivan with the seats down

## Open Questions
- [ ] Ping pong ball durability — will they crack after a full day of play? Bring 2 extra packs ($8) just in case
- [ ] Damper valve sweet spot — needs real-world testing. May need to try 2-3 valve positions before the event
- [ ] Fog machine outdoor wind — natural breeze may dissipate fog too quickly outdoors. Test in actual booth conditions
- [ ] Boom arm friction — PVC-on-PVC may be too slippery (arm swings too freely) or too sticky. May need a Teflon tape wrap or a light grease for smooth pivoting
- [ ] Debris containment — 8" walls should catch most debris, but some will escape. Accept it or add a mesh lip
